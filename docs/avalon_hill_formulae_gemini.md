**INDIVIDUAL CARDS**
In an effort to cut down production costs and also time in getting the cards ready. there is no reason to offer as many players as in the past. Football is unlike baseball where every batter faces a singular pitcher in football it is more often an offense versus a defense with strategy involved. Therefore, each team should have the following individual players rated: WIDE RECEIVERS - 5, OFFENSIVE LINEMEN8, QUARTERBACKS - 3, RUNNING BACKS - 6, TIGIIT ENDS-3, DEFENSIVE LINEMEN - 6, LINEBACKERS - 8, DEFENSE BACKS-7 This makes each roster contain 45 individual player cards, and that is certainly a sufficient number. In making such a plan known, it will also cut down on the number of complaints that you did not include Josef Stalin who played 2 games at free safety in October. On the Roster or Depth Sheet, as I write In the players. I add two notations an X for Interceptions, and an S for sacks. As an example, if John Doe had four interceptions and made 2 sacks, then next to his name. write in. "X4, S2." This speeds up the process when rating cards as two areas are dependent on individual interceptions and sacks.
