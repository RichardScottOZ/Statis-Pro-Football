# STATS-PRO FOOTBALL

**The Avalon Hill Game Company's Trademark for its game of Professional Football**

## 5TH EDITION RULES

## INTRODUCTION

STATIS-PRO FOOTBALL is a man-to-man simulation of pro football based upon actual statistical performances in the NFL over the past season.

## EQUIPMENT

1. Football Field: 2 Offensive, 2 Defensive Displays
2. Rules Folder
3. Scorepad
4. Football and First Down Marker
5. Player Cards and Fast Action Cards

## HOW TO PLAY

Unless otherwise stated, all rules of pro football apply to the game. Adhere to the following sequence when resolving each play:

1. **Substitution:** The offense and defense may freely substitute players on their respective squads as long as no more than 11 players are on a Display at a given time.

2. **Offense/Defense:** The defensive player secretly chooses a defense and strategy; the offensive player secretly chooses a play, player and strategy.

3. **Formation:** The defense may adjust the formation of its players.

4. **Play Revelation:** Both players reveal their calls. If a blitz is in effect, the defense must remove a certain number of players from its Display at this time. (These are the players involved in the Blitz.)

5. **Resolution:** The play is resolved.

6. **Time:** The amount of time expended by the play is noted on the scorepad.

7. **Two-Minute Offense:** The offense may declare that it is commencing a "two minute offense."

## DISPLAYS

Both players receive Displays on which their offensive and defense squads are represented. No Display may ever have more than 11 players on it at any given time.

### 1. Offensive Display:

Both players must always maintain FIVE linemen on their Offensive Displays: two tackles, two guards, and one center. TWO ends must also be maintained at all times - one Left and one Right. According to the whim of the owning player, these ends may be either Tight Ends or Wide Receivers. One Quarterback must be on the Display at all times. In addition, both players must maintain at least one, and up to three, running backs (labeled, "BK1," "BK2," and "BK3"). Finally, the offense has the option of employing another wide receiver as a flanker. The number of wide receivers maintained on the Offensive Display is dependent on the number of running backs being employed. If three running backs are being used, then one back must occupy the flanker position. If two backs are in use, a single wide receiver may be used as a flanker. If only one running back is being utilized, then a second wide receiver or tight end may assume the open running back slot, but must be designated as "Flanker-2." Note that only one player may be placed per box on the Offensive Display. Also note that receivers are categorized as either "WR" or "TE" on the individual player cards.

### 2. Defensive Display:

The Defensive Display consists of 15 boxes, subdivided into three rows of five boxes apiece. Row 1 is the Defensive Line; Row 2 is the Linebacker area; Row 3 is the secondary or Defensive Back alignment.

**Row 1:** At least three, and up to ten cards, must be maintained in Row 1. Zero, one or two players may be placed per box. Only ends, tackles and linebackers can occupy any boxes in Row 1. These boxes are labeled A, B, C, D and E.

**Row 2:** From zero to five players must be maintained in Row 2, all of which must be Linebackers. Only one player per box, and the boxes are labeled F, G, H, I and J.

**Row 3:** From zero to six players must be maintained in Row 3, all of which must be Defensive Backs (safeties or cornerbacks). These boxes are labeled K, L, M, N and O. Only one player may be placed inside each box, EXCEPT FOR BOX L, in which zero, one or two players MAY BE positioned. Cornerbacks must be placed in Boxes K and O, the free safety must be located in Box M; and the strong or tight safety in Box N. Any defensive back may be placed in Box L, but not until every other box in Row 3 has already been occupied.

**NOTE:** The defensive player may change the arrangement of his players on the Display BEFORE both players reveal their calls on the chosen play.

## PLAYS

Nine plays are available to the offense: five runs and four passes. The runs are Sweep Left (SL), Sweep Right (SR), Inside Left (IL), Inside Right (IR), and End-Around (ER). The passes are Quick (QK), Short (SH), Long (LG) and Screen (SC). Each offensive play must signify the use of an offensive player(s) currently on the Display. An End-Around can only be called when an on-Display receiver possesses a Rushing Column. When a pass is being chosen as the play, the coach must name the intended receiver - which can be any non-lineman except the Quarterback. A long pass may not be called when the scrimmage line is within the opponent's 20-yard-line, and a screen pass cannot be called within the 5-yard-line.

There are seven plays available to the defense. Four are Run Defenses, "keying" on one of three specific backs or, on no back at all. One is a Pass Defense. One is a Prevent Defense, and finally, the Blitz.

**How to Blitz:** If the defense calls a Blitz, this must be announced immediately BEFORE the offense reveals its play call. At this time, the defense must remove at least two, and up to five, linebacks or defensive backs from the Display. These are the players chosen to blitz the passer. (They are placed back on the Display after the current play has been fully resolved.)

When picking a play or defense, players either secretly mark the appropriate circle on the Play Selection Chart, or use the proper play cards (1987 edition).

## FAST ACTION CARDS

There are 109 Fast Action Cards ("FAC") in the game: 13 of these are special "Z" cards, and the remainder are "normal" cards. Before the start of play, the FAC deck should be thoroughly shuffled and placed, facedown, near the board. Then, as plays are undertaken, the players will flip the topmost card over, revealing its informational side. After this card is used, it is temporarily discarded after which the next FAC is flipped over. One card is used for ONE READING or Result. This process continues until all of the FAC have been used, after which the deck is, once again, thoroughly reshuffled, and used in the same fashion.

Every FAC contains a Play Directive for each offensive play call. When resolving a play, the appropriate directive of the first FAC flipped will tell the players how to begin to determine the result of that play. Sometimes, the players will have to consult the FAC's numbers, of which there are two numbers on each card: a Run Number and a Pass Number. Run Numbers are from 1 to 12, Pass Numbers from 1 to 48.

## RUNNING PLAYS

Resolve runs according to the following procedure:

1. The topmost FAC is flipped and appropriate play under "RUNS" is referred to. This will direct the run to: (a) an offensive player; (b) a defensive box; (c) an offensive player versus a defensive box. Although abbreviations are listed at the end of this rules booklet, it is helpful to remember that play directives always list offensive players with a two-letter abbreviation (eg., LG, BK, CN) and defensive boxes with a one- letter abbreviation (e.g., A, E, I).

2. The next FAC is flipped and its Run Number is referred to. Note that this number may be modified (see "Run Number Modifiers").

3. The modified Run Number is applied to the Rush Column of the back who is carrying the ball. Next to this number will be a Yardage Figure indicating the number of yards gained or lost on the play.

4. If, in Step 1, the run was directed to an offensive player or players, this player's Blocking Value (BV) modifies the yardage result. Add or subtract the BV from the yardage, yielding a final result on the play.

5. If, in Step 1, the run was directed to a defensive box or boxes, this box should be checked to see if it is occupied by any players. If so, the yardage result obtained in Step 3 is modified by adding or subtracting this player(s)'s Tackle Value (TV) from the yardage, yielding a final result.

6. If, in Step 1, the run was directed to an offensive player versus a defensive box, add the offense BV of the player to the defensive player's TV; if the offensive player's BV is a NEGATIVE number, then subtract his BV from the defensive player's TV. When the result is ZERO, neither player's value comes into play, and the yardage obtained in Step 3 is the Final Yardage on the Play. If the result is a positive number, the offensive player has won the "trench or blocking battle," and his ORIGINAL BV (not the sum of the BV and TV) is added to the play's yardage as described in Step 4. If the result is a negative number, the defensive player has won the "blocking battle," and his ORIGINAL TV is subtracted from the play's yardage as described in Step 5.

**Special Cases:** If a play is directed to two offensive players or two defensive boxes, add up the BVs or TVs of the indicated players before modifying the plays' yardage as described in Steps 4 and 5. However, any time a play is directed at a defensive box that is unoccupied, add TWO YARDS to the result obtained in Step 3 (if a play is directed at two boxes, both would have to be unoccupied for the two-yard bonus to be implemented). If the FAC calls for an offensive player versus a defensive box that is empty, do not award this two-yard bonus; instead, simply add that offensive player's BV to the yardage. Finally, if a play is directed at a defensive box that is occupied by TWO PLAYERS, consider that box to be occupied by a single player with a TV of MINUS 4 (-4), regardless of the TVs of the two players in the box.

**Example:** An Inside Left run, directed at boxes BG by the first FAC, gains 1-yard. However, in Box B is a tackle with a TV of (-2), and in Box G is a linebacker with a TV of (-3). Thus, the final yardage of the play is 0 (1 minus 2 plus 1). If both boxes had been unoccupied, the final yardage would have been 3 (1+2). If the linebacker had been moved in Box B before the play, in expectation of a run, the final yardage would have been minus 3 (-4). If the first FAC had stated, "LG vs B," the players would have had to add the BV of the left guard (say +3) to the TV of the tackle in Box B. The result is a positive number, so the guard wins the "blocking battle," and 3 is added to the play's result, making it a gain of 4-yards.

**Run Number Modifiers:** There is no modification to the Run Number if a Pass, Prevent or Blitz defense is in effect. However, if a back is keyed, correctly, with a Run Defense being used, FOUR IS ADDED to the Run Number obtained from the next FAC, as explained in Step 2. (Keying is performed by the defensive coach, stipulating which back he thinks will carry the ball.) It is not mandatory to key on every play. If any back carries the ball against a Run Defense with no keying, TWO is added to the Run Number. If a back is carrying the ball against a Run Defense that is keying on another back, the Run Number is not modified. No Run Number may be decreased below 1 or increased above 12. SEE THE RUN NUMBER MODIFIERS TABLE FOR A SUMMARY OF ALL MODIFIERS.

**Short Gains:** Each player has been given individual ratings for rushing - under columns headed by N, SG and LG. N, standing for Normal, is used to resolve most rushing results, whereas SG, short gain, comes up when "I" is called for under Column N. When SG is in effect, obtain a new FAC Run Number from 1 to 12, and obtain the rush yardage from the SG column. As an example, if a ball carrier has been directed to use FAC Run Number 3, then the yardage will be given across from "3" on the player's card. However, if the Run Number had been "1," then a new FAC Run Number would be obtained, and a new yardage figure found in the SG Column. A few players, with very low rushing grades, will not have a Short Gain rating.

**Long Gains or Breakaway:** The only time that the LG, Long Gain Column, on the individual card is used is when the first drawn FAC relates, BREAK - meaning that instead of any blocking or tackling values, the running back has found daylight. He is on a breakaway run, and you get a yardage by obtaining a new Run Number and applying it to the LG Column.

**End-Around Run:** The offense may call an End-Around or Reverse Play only if a tight end or wide receiver, currently on Display, possesses a Rush column. A particular player may only have an End-Around play directed to him ONCE per game. To resolve an End-Around, consult the "ER" information on the first FAC card flipped. The result will either be the letters, "OK," or a negative yardage figure. If "OK" appears, the End-Around may be resolved through the procedure for any rushing play, obtaining a Run Number from the next FAC, and modifying it, if necessary, and locating the number under the N column. If Run Number 1 is called for, the SG column comes into play, and a new Run Number is applied to the SG column. If Run Number 1 or 12 is drawn for the SG Column, you employ the LG column - using a new Run Number. Receivers are the only ball carriers that get Long Gains directly from their own rushing columns. Running Backs obtain Long Gains only when BREAK appears on a FAC. If, when checking the ER information on the FAC, a NEGATIVE number appears, that is the automatic yardage lost on the play, and no more resolution is needed.

**Maximum Losses:** The maximum yardage loss on an Inside Run is THREE YARDS. There is no limit to the number of yards that can be lost on a Sweep.

**Blocking Backs:** Some FAC may direct a run to a back ("BK") rather than a lineman or defensive box. This means that the back who is not carrying the ball uses his blocking ability or value (BV) to add or subtract yardage from the play, as described in Step 4. If there is no back on Display, other than the one carrying the ball, no modifications are made. If there are TWO backs on Display, in addition to the one who is carrying the ball, both BVs are coupled, and added to the Run Number yardage.

## PASSING PLAYS

Resolve Quick, Short, and Long Passes according to the following procedure:

1. The topmost FAC is flipped and the Passes section is consulted. Next to the word, ALL, an offensive position or abbreviation, "ORIG," (for Original) indicated. "Orig" means that the pass directed to the player originally indicate by the offensive player at the start of the play. An offensive position mean that the original receiver on the play is too heavily guarded, and the quarterback is forced to "check off to a secondary receiver." Exception: If a pass is directed to a position that unoccupied at the moment, the pass said to have been thrown away incomplete.

2. The next FAC is flipped and its Pass Number is referred to. The quarterback's Passing Column is consulted - in particular, the type of pass that is being attempted (Quick, Short or Long). The possible results are: Complete (COM), Incomplete (INC) or interception (INT).

**Completion Range:** For all non-Screen Passes, each quarterback has Completion Range. This is simply a range of Pass Numbers on the quarterback's card under the heading "COM" (Complete). On all passes, the Completion Range is modified by TV FACTORS: 1) Defense currently in effect, and 2) Pass Defense Value of the defensive player (if any) guarding the receiver to which the pass is ultimate directed.

**Defenses and Passes:** Consult the Defense/Pass Table (Page 5) to determine the effect of each defense on the Completion Range of Quick, Short and Long Passes. Note that some defenses increase in effectiveness when the scrimmage line is within 20-yards of the defense's goal.

**Pass Defense:** Defensive player occupying certain boxes MUST guard specific receivers during a pass. Additional defenders may participate this guarding, if a Double or Triple Coverage strategy is in effect (see "Strategies"). Pass Defense assignments are as follows:

- RE is guarded by BOX N
- LE is guarded by BOX K
- FL#1 is guarded by BOX O
- FL#2 is guarded by BOX M
- BK#1 is guarded by BOX F
- BK#2 is guarded by BOX J
- BK#3 is guarded by BOX H

When a pass is being resolved, add or subtract the Pass Defense Value of the defender guarding the receiver to which the pass is being directed. However, if a box is empty and a pass is directed to a player that is normal guarded by this box, FIVE is automatically added to the quarterback Completion Range when resolving the pass.

**Incomplete Passes:** If a Pass Number to 48) falls within the Incomplete (INC) range on a quarterback's card, the pass is incomplete and the play is over. No that if a quarterback's Completion Range is reduced due to modifiers, the numbers that were formerly complete are converted to incomplete passes.

**Complete Passes:** If a Pass Number falls within the Complete (COM) range on a quarterback's card, the pass Complete. The receiver's card referred to and a new FAC is flipping Consult the Run Number and cross reference it with the Pass Gain section of the receiver, next to the type of pass being executed (Q=Quick, S=Short, L=Long). This will yield a yardage figure, which is the number of yards gained on the play. Exception: If the receiver's card does not yield a yardage figure, but is simply blank, this means the pass has been dropped, and the play Has resulted in an incomplete pass attempt.

**Long Gains:** Each receiver is given three columns of gain data - quick, short and long. Use the proper column to determine the length of the gain. If in the last two minutes of a game, any long pass that is completed to a wide receiver will find Random Number "1" being changed to a TOUCHDOWN. Other numbers remain as is.

**Interception:** If a Pass Number falls within the Intercepted (INT) range on a quarterback's card, the pass is intercepted by a defensive player. Consult the Interception Table to determine who intercepts the pass, flipping a new FAC and using its Run Number to do so.

If a Pass Number falls within the Incomplete (INC) range on a quarterback's card, the pass may still be intercepted if this Number falls within the Intercept Range of the defensive player's card who is guarding the receiver to which the pass had ultimately been directed (for "guarding assignments, see "Pass Defense").

NOTE: If Double Coverage is in effect on a receiver, the defenders must include the normal defender plus any SINGLE player in Boxes L or M (defense's choice). If Triple Coverage is in effect, the defenders must include the normal defender plus any TWO players from Boxes G, H, I, L or M (again, defensive choice). In Double Coverage, any assigned defender is eligible to intercept the pass. If more than one player can intercept, the defense chooses which one to intercept with.

**Interception-48?:** Many players possess an Interception Rating of "48?", signifying that they MAY or MAY NOT have intercepted the pass. When the Pass Number is 48 and a "48?" appears, obtain a new Pass Number, and if it is between 1 and 24, the interception has been made, 25-48 means that the pass was deflected away, incomplete.

**Interception Returns:** Before the interception is returned, the Point of Interception (PI) must be determined. This is the number of yards, downfield, at which the interception return begins. Flip a new FAC, determine its Run Number, and consult this chart:

**SCREEN PASS-One half of run number**
**QUICK PASS-Use the run number**
**SHORT PASS-Run number times TWO**
**LONG PASS-Run number times FOUR**

If the PI is on the goal line or in the end zone, no return takes place and a touchdown occurs. (Ball changes hands, and is placed in play on the 20-yard-line.) If an interception is to be returned, consult the Interception Return Table, determine the position of the interceptor (lineman, linebacker or secondary) and flip a new FAC looking at its Run Number. Cross-reference this Number to the proper column of the Return Table to determine the interception return yardage from the PI (Point of Interception).

**Pass Rushes:** Sometimes when a pass is being resolved, the first FAC flipped will not direct the pass to a receiver, but will instead state, PASS RUSH. If a PASS RUSH occurs, ignore the normal method for resolving the pass; instead, follow this procedure:

1. The defense adds up the Pass Rush Values of all players in Row 1. If a Blitz is in effect, all blitzing players have a Pass Rush Value of 2 regardless of their printed values, and are added into this total.

2. Offense adds up the Pass Blocking Values of his five linemen.

3. The Sums obtained in Steps 1 and 2 are compared. If the defensive sum is greater, the difference between the sums is multiplied by TWO and ADDED to the quarterback's SACK Range of numbers on the PASS RUSH line on his player's card. If the offensive sum is greater, the difference between the two sums is multiplied by TWO and subtracted from the quarterback's Sack Range. If there is no difference between the sums, the quarterback's Sack Range is unchanged.

4. A new FAC is flipped and its Pass Number is consulted. Apply this number to the PASS RUSH line on the quarterback's card. The possible results are SACK (see "Sacks"), RUN (the offense flips a new FAC, determines its Run Number, and consults the quarterback's Rush column for the yardage on the play), COM (the pass is completed to the intended receiver), and INC (the pass is incomplete). Note that a quarterback's Completion Range on his Pass Rush line is never altered for any reason - be it for the type of defense in use, or the Pass Value of defenders. However, if his Sack Range is altered (see Step 3), numbers that were formally Sacks are changed to RUNS instead. During a Pass Rush, a pass may only be intercepted if: a) the pass is incomplete on the Pass Rush line; and b) the Pass Number is within the Intercept Range of the defender of the intended receiver.

**Blitzes:** If a Blitz is in effect and a Short or Long Pass has been called, a Pass Rush automatically occurs. Ignore the normal pass procedure and follow the above Steps. However, Quick and Screen Passes would be resolved, normally.

**Sacks:** If a quarterback is sacked, flip a new FAC and refer to its Pass Number. Divide this number by THREE (round fractions upward), and the result is the number of yards lost on the play.

## STRATEGIES

The offense has four strategies available on each play and the defense has two. Each strategy may only be called under certain conditions, although their use is never mandatory.

### 1. Offensive Strategies

**a. Flop:** This may only be called with an Inside Run to the quarterback. It automatically results in a loss of 1-yard - no FAC are flipped and no fumble is possible.

**b. Sneak:** This may only be called with an Inside Run to the quarterback. A single FAC is flipped - if the Pass Number is EVEN, the play gains 1-yard; if the Pass Number is ODD, the play gains no yards (0). If the first FAC yields a "Z", flip another card to resolve the sneak and then determine the meaning of the "Z" normally.

**c. Draw Play:** This may only be called with an Inside Run to any back or the quarterback. If a Pass or Prevent Defense is in effect, subtract TWO from the Run Number when resolving the run (subtract FOUR against a Blitz). However, add TWO if any Run Defense is in effect. These modifiers are in addition to normal Run Number modifiers called for on each particular defense.

**d. Play-Action:** This may only be called with a Short or Long Pass. If any Run Defense is in effect, add FIVE to the quarterback's Completion Range. If a Pass Defense has been called, subtract FIVE. If a Prevent Defense is in effect, subtract TEN.

### 2. Defensive Strategies

**a. Double Coverage:** This may only be called if a Pass or Prevent Defense is in effect AND a) Four Players occupy both Row-2 and Row-3 or; b) Three Players occupy Row-2 and five players occupy Row-3. When revealing a Double Coverage call, the defensive player must specify who it is being applied against (LE, RE, FL#1, FL#2, BK#1, BK#2 or BK#3) ALOUD - before the offense reveals its play! If a receiver to which a pass has been thrown is double covered, ignore the Pass Defense Value of the normal defender. Instead, he is automatically considered covered with a combined Pass Defense of MINUS 7 (i.e., subtract 7 from the QB's Completion Range) regardless of the printed Values of the defenders.

**b. Triple Coverage:** This may only be called if a Pass or Prevent Defense is in effect and two players occupy Row-2 and six players occupy Row-3. Specify aloud who is being triple covered. If a receiver to which a pass is being thrown is triple covered, ignore the Pass Defense Value of the normal defender. Instead, he is automatically considered covered by a combined Pass Defense Value of MINUS 15 (i.e., subtract 15 from the QB's Completion Range). NOTE: If the defense meets the conditions for triple coverage, he may instead state, aloud, that he is double covering TWO specific receivers.

## KICKING

### 1. Punts:

If the offense wishes to punt, this fact is stated, aloud. A FAC is flipped and its Run Number referred to. This number is applied to the team's Punter Card, the result indicating the yardage of the punt and basic instructions for its return. "FC" means fair catch and no return is made. "PR-1 to PR-4" indicates the return man who will run back the punt. To return a punt, flip a new FAC and refer to its Run Number. This number is applied to the proper column of the Punt Return Card, the result indicating the return yardage. If the result is followed by an asterisk, a new FAC is flipped, and a new number of 1 or 2 means that the asterisked result at the bottom of the card is the "correct" return yardage instead of the original number. A new number of 3-12 means to use the original yardage obtained. An "f" after a result means that the return is fumbled at its conclusion (see "Fumbles"). If a penalty occurs due to a result off a punter's card, flip a new FAC and refer to its run number. An even number indicates a 5-yard movement penalty against the kicking team, and an odd number indicates a 5-yard movement penalty against the return team. These are automatic penalties and cannot be declined. The punt is then restaged, following the same format.

**Punt Number 12:** When Number 12 appears on the punter's card, it always signifies that a new 1-12 number be obtained, and this new number will bring about either the punter's longest kick.

### Coffin Corner Punts:

The punter may state that any punt is a "Coffin Corner" attempt. This means that the punting player must state a number of yards (at least 10 and no more than 25) that he wishes to automatically deduct from the punt's yardage. The pre-stated yardage must be applied no matter what the punt's result is. If the Run Number resolving the punt is odd, the punt goes out of bounds and no return is possible. If the number is even, the punt is returned normally, unless a fair catch ("FC") results. A FC cannot be made inside the five, and if this is the result - consider the punt to have gone into the end zone for a touchback. In non-Coffin Corner punts, any punts that land inside the opponent's six yard line are considered as having rolled into the end zone, and no return is made.

### All-Out Punt Rush:

On any punt, the defense may state that it is attempting an "All-Out Rush." Such a punt would be resolved normally, except ignore all results on the punter's card next to Run Number 12. If a 12 is revealed, flip a new FAC and obtain a new Run Number. Numbers 1-4 indicate that the punt is blocked, and the blocking team recovers 15-yards behind the line of scrimmage. Numbers 5-9 indicate that the punt is not blocked, had to be kicked hurriedly, and use the yardage across from number 11. A Run Number of 10-12 indicates that the punter was hit by the all-out rush, and the defense is charged with "Roughing the Punter," a 15-yard penalty that also provides the kicking team with a new First Down. The maximum return on any punt with an all-out rush is THREE YARDS.

## Z CARDS

13 cards in the Fast Action Deck are "Z CARDS." These cards call for special events, such as fumbles, penalties or injuries. Z cards are only applicable if they are revealed during the first THREE FAC that are flipped in order to resolve a play. That is, a Z card is ignored (and a new FAC is flipped) if it is revealed after the third FAC flipped to resolve a play. Z cards are also ignored when: a) A second (or third, fourth, etc) Z card is revealed on the same play (i.e. a maximum of ONE Z CARD is taken into account each play - the first one); b) On onsite kicks, extra points, and fumble recovery determinations.

When a Z card is applicable to the play, finish resolving the play normally. Then, determine the meaning of the Z card by flipping a new FAC and reading the section labeled, Z RESULTS.

### 1. Penalties ("PEN"):

If a penalty is called for, the category of that penalty must be determined: #1 applies on running plays, Quick and Screen Passes, and field goal attempts; #2 applies on Short and Long Passes; #3 applies on punts and their returns. Each type of penalty is followed by a capital letter and a number. The letter stands for the team the penalty is applied against: (O. Offense; D. Defense; K. Kicking; R. Receiving) and the number refers to the type of penalty that has been called. Refer to the Penalty Table to determine the meaning of this number. Most penalties leave the non-penalized team the option of accepting or declining the (and thus accepting the play's result and down expenditure); however, there are a few penalties that are labeled no option, and these MUST BE ACCEPTED.

### 2. Injuries ("INJ"):

If an injury occurs, the FAC specifies whether it applies to an offensive position or a defensive box. If two defenders occupy an indicated box, then both are injured, but if the box is empty then nobody is injured. Some injuries are labeled "BC"; this stands for "ball carrier" and refers to the player who was carrying the ball when the play ended. When an injury occurs, a new FAC should be flipped and its Pass Number referred to. Consult the Injury Table to determine the length of the injury. Injuries cannot take place on any kicking plays. Ignore "INJ, BC" results on kicks and incomplete passes. If a position has already been hit by an injury," a second injury to that position is ignored. However, once the formerly injured player is eligible to return to the Display, any future injuries will be enforced.

### 3. Fumbles:

If a fumble occurs, flip a new FAC and determine its Pass Number. Apply this number to the fumbling team's roster card, locating their FUMBLES LOST heading, and adjusting that number, either up or down, with the Defensive Team's Defensive (Def) Fumble Adjustment (Adj). As an example, if the offense has a Fumble Lost rating of 1-21 and the Defense has an adjustment of +3, the new fumbles lost range is 1-24. All fumbles are recovered at the exact spot that the play ended. If a "Fumble(S)" appears on a Z Card, a normal fumble takes place unless the ball carrier is on the home team. If he is, no fumble occurs. (This is an attempt to build in the home field to a very slight advantage, as it actually is in the NFL). Ignore fumbles that take place on field goal attempts, plays that involve touchdowns, and incomplete passes.

## TIMING

A game consists of four quarters, each of 15 minutes duration. Each play expends a certain number of seconds that are recorded on the scorepad. A penciled "X" in a box represents 10-seconds of time; a half-"X" (or slash) represents 5-seconds. Consult the Timing Table to determine the time expenditure of various plays.

### Out of Bounds:

If the last FAC resolving a play uses its Run Number and that Run Number is followed by the letters "OB," the play ends out of bounds. Exception: Inside Runs may never end up out of bounds.

### Time Outs:

Each player receives three times out per half. A time out may only be called after a play that takes more than 10-seconds. The clock automatically stops when there are exactly TWO MINUTES to play in the 2nd and 4th quarters (even if a play is run that would normally bring the clock below the two-minute mark).

### Two Minute Offense:

The offense, usually late in the half or fourth quarter, can invoke the Two-Minute Offense. This offense can be used in the fourth quarter, prior to the two minute warning only if the team trails by 20 or more points. The decision may be rescinded at the end of any play, voluntarily, and is automatically rescinded when the ball changes possession. It is never in effect on any kicking plays. Each play run with a Two-Minute Offense uses up ONE-HALF of its normal time expenditure but is penalized in the following ways: a) On a run or Screen Pass, its final yardage is halved (round fractions upward), although a play that would normally be a touchdown (for negative yardage plays) are unaffected. b) Non-Screen passes have the quarterback's Completion Range reduced by four in addition to other normal modifiers. Finally, any non-Inside Run and all pass plays that have their yardage determined by an EVEN Run Number automatically end up out of bounds. Such plays that are resolved by ODD Run Numbers stay in bounds (unless they go out of bounds normally). Incomplete passes are never affected by any out of bounds considerations.

### Special Rules:

No play may expend more time than is necessary to bring a quarter to a close. No half may end with a defensive penalty. If such a penalty occurs on the last play of a half (first or second half), an additional play is rewarded to the offense, and play continues until no time remains on the clock. A half may end on an Offensive Penalty.

If a team possess two quarterbacks with an "A" Endurance, either may start the game, and they can be substituted, freely. ## SOLITAIRE PLAY

All standard rules apply to solitaire play except:

A. Offensive plays are chosen by the player. Defenses are determined by the FAC.

B. Remove one Z card from the FAC deck.

C. The offense may not choose two Screen or two Quick Passes in succession.

D. Before resolving each play, flip a FAC and refer to the Solitaire section. This area has five possible defenses listed, one of which will be the defense for that particular Play. Each defense is given a number from 1 to 5. Each number applies to a specific game situation:

**Number 1.** All plays that are run on FIRST DOWN

**Number 2.** All plays on Second Down with less than 6 yards to go for a first down or any plays when the ball is on the opponent's 3, 4 or 5- yard-line, regardless of down.

**Number 3.** All plays on Second Down with 7 or more yards needed for a first down

**Number 4.** All plays on Third and Fourth down with 7 or more yards needed for a first down

**Number 5.** All plays on Third or Fourth Down with 6 or less yards needed for a first down or when the ball is on the opponent's 1 or 2-yard- line, regardless of down.
